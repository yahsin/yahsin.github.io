Where did 14 Indicators Come From?

To elicit and characterize characteristics of AE in infoVis, we conducted a lab study where we asked our participants to interact with visualizations, and captured participants' engagement as a phenomena via verbal protocols, eye tracking, behavioral indicators, and self-assessments.

After the data is collected, three qualitative coding method are used in this stage: process coding, emotion coding and causation coding (Saldana, 2009).

We used process coding to identify the events or activities carried out through the session. Emotion coding is used for identifying user's emotion and their affective reactions during the session. Finally the causation coding is used to identify the “chains” of previous two codes and find out causal relationship between them.

Based on the observation, we conduct a qualitative analysis based on grounded theory. By concluding codes from three coding methods, we propose 14 elements of affective engagement in the following table.

Items (questions in the survey instrument) were written based on the notes during qualitative coding process, and there would be multiple candidates for each of the indicator. Due to the fact that not all items will be presented in the actual survey, the purpose of this expert review is to help us select and revise current items from item bank to help us to construct an appropriate instrument.

Indicators Description
Fluidity The flow of use should be continuous, smooth, not disrupted
Enthusiasm The user is interested, eager, proactively involved, and motivated
Curiosity The user is inquisitive, investigative, ...to do something
Discovery The user discovers something new or interesting along the way
Seeking investigating, searching, testing during the interaction with a visualization
Expressiveness The visualization expresses a clear concept or message; the data is clear
Persuasion The visualization communicates a powerful message, impacts or influences the user
Storytelling The visualization tells a story or has a narrative style
Creativity The visualization helps users generate or express creative thoughts or ideas
Novelty The visualization displays an innovative presentation or point of view
Entertaining The visualization is fun or charming
Control The user feels in control and doesn't feel upset (negative emotion)
Captivation The user is concentrating, immersed, and forgets about surroundings
Visually Pleasing The user is impressed by the visualization

Reference

Saldana, J. (2009). The coding manual for qualitative researchers. Los Angeles, [Calif.] ; London: SAGE.
